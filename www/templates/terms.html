Disputes (Resolutions) Policy

Overview

Generally, transactions are smooth on Swaap. However, there may be some cases where buyers and sellers face issues. At Swaap, we have a dispute resolution process in order to resolve disputes between buyers and sellers.

What is a dispute?

A dispute can be defined as a serious disagreement between a buyer and a seller in connection with a transaction on the Website and/or App.

How disputes happen in a barter system?

Disputes are filed as the result of disagreements between buyers and sellers. Disputes can be raised by either party with the other when complaints/issues are not resolved in their favor.

It is important that before buyers raise disputes, Buyer and Seller should attempt to resolve the issues between themselves.

How a dispute is created?

Whenever there is a disagreement, a buyer and seller can write an email to helpdesk@swaap.in in order to raise a dispute. A dispute can be raised at a particular transaction level.

What are the various disputes?

The following are some indicative examples of potential disputes:

1. wrong item received;

2. item not as described;

a) damaged or seal-broken product;

b) part/accessory missing;

c) item not compatible;

d) seller description/specification wrong;

e) defective (functional issues); and

f) product not working and manufacturer says invalid invoice.

In case a seller rejects the return request of a buyer, and the buyer raises a dispute, then Swaap shall try and mediate between the seller and the buyer for a resolution of the dispute. If the dispute is resolved in favor of the buyer, then the buyer shall be entitled to a replacement/refund provided the buyer returns the product to the seller. If the dispute is settled in favor of the seller, the buyer shall not be entitled to any refund/replacement.

• your listing/title contains a word or phrase that does not seem to belong to the item listed/offered for sale; and

• a buyer cannot figure out which item you have listed/offered for sale from the title.

Violations of this policy may result in a range of actions, including:

• Listing cancellation
• Limits placed on account privileges
• Loss of special status
• Account suspension

Search Manipulation Policy

Search manipulation is any practice that results in searches on the Website and/or App returning irrelevant listings or that otherwise inhibit a User's ability to accurately locate specific items. It shall be a violation of Swaap’s policy and may result in listing removal or account suspension. This violation most often consists of the use of brand names or other inappropriate ‘keywords’ for the purpose of gaining attention or diverting users to a listing. Search manipulation is unfair to Users who may be searching for a specific item and receive inaccurate search results. Certain uses of brand names may also constitute trademark infringement and could expose sellers to legal liability.

Types of Search Manipulation

Keyword Spamming

Keyword spamming is the practice of including brand names or other inappropriate ‘keywords’ in a title or description for the purpose of gaining attention or diverting users to a listing. Keyword spamming is unfair to users who may be searching for a specific item and receive search results of listings not related to the item. Users often confused and frustrated by such tactics.

Types of Search Manipulation

Keyword Spamming

Swaap Rule(s) -- All words used to describe an item, whether in the title, description of the item page, or any other way that may be found using search, must be strictly related to the item being sold. In addition, items contained in a listing as bonuses or gifts may not be described in the title.

Hidden Text -- Sellers shall not be permitted to include hidden text (for example ‘white on white’) or source tags (meta or header tags). Keywords ‘hidden’ within the title, description, or through other means are not allowed. Such deliberate attempts may result in account suspension without prior warning.

Brand Name Misuse -- Do not include any brand name or company logo in your listing other than the specific brand name used by the company that has manufactured or produced the item you are listing. Certain uses of brand names may also constitute trademark infringement and could expose you to legal liability. Sellers are permitted to use third-party company logos only if they have the license or permission to use such logos.

Comparisons -- Do not compare items in your listings or titles. On the Website and/or App, sellers are not permitted to make comparisons between items in a listing title.

Misleading Titles -- Make sure your title accurately describes only the actual item or items you offer for sale. If your title contains a word or phrase that does not seem to belong to the item(s), make sure it is clear from your description why you included that word or phrase in the title.

Warning: Listings that include unrelated brand names or logos may be ended early by Swaap. Multiple violations of Swaap’s policy could result in the suspension of your account.
